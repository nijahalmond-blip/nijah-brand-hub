Belief logic

Every piece installs one belief: B1 believe in Nijah, B2 believe in the vehicle (GRIT / Next Chapter Experience), B3 believe a woman like me can do it. Pillars 1-3 build B1 and B2. At least one post per batch must sit in pillar 4 or 5 and build B3 with real client proof, or the audience believes in Nijah but not in themselves. That is the gap between follower and buyer.

Channel priority

LinkedIn is channel #1. Podcast guesting is #2. YouTube is #3. Substack Notes and the newsletter feed the top of funnel. Lead with lived, cinematic specifics ("Third floor. Budget review. Slides I had seen seventeen times.") not "high-performing women often feel."

Words & phrases

Stop performing the woman who got you here.

Become the author of your own life again.
A life that feels as true as it looks successful.

Use with intent

alignment, clarity, identity, self-trust, reinvent, recommit, self-authored, next chapter, performance, truth, certainty, grief, permission. Purpose and journey are now allowed for this body of work.

Still banned

hustle, motivation, mindset, empower. Also avoid vague promises like "discover your authentic self." Name the elephant: "the identity crisis success never prepared you for."

Content direction

Lead from Nijah's lived story so the reader sees herself through her, not just learns from her. Open loops, sustain them, close them with the exact promised truth. No pivot mid-piece. Sell the symptom in public, reveal the diagnosis inside.

The hook to test as core message

"You do not need another leadership program. You do not need to push harder. The woman who created your success may not be the woman you want to become. In the GRIT Method, we close that gap, because you cannot optimize your way out of an identity problem."
